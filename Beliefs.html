Six Dimensions of Belief:
Our research found that LDS beliefs vary across six dimensions, each of which are distinct but interconnected. Together, they represent a framework to understand how different people within the church believe in different ways and why.

Ecclesiastical Authority
The degree to which individuals defer to, trust in, and prioritize church leadership, institutional structures, and prophetic authority.
Social Progressivism
The extent to which individuals support adapting Church teachings, policies, and culture to align with progressive social values.
Doctrinal Heterodoxy
The extent to which individuals believe in unorthodox doctrines, having an openness to evolving, flexible, contextual, and less rigid conceptions of LDS doctrine and teachings.
Pacifism
The strength of an individual's aversion to violence and their opposition to the justification of force, even dire need.
Conventional Orthopraxy
The extent to which individuals rigidly adhere to conventional practices and standards, resisting changes or challenges to established norms.
Self-Directed Religiosity
The extent to which an individual's religious practices, spirituality, and belief systems are personally determined, rather than derived from institutional interpretation or convention.
